    Overview

    This was a 10-week concept project with mentorship from a Google PM where our team explored one deceptively simple question: how can Photos help people feel closer, not just store pictures?

    In our small, design-driven team, I worked on the product strategy side ‚Äî helping narrow a broad "human connection online" challenge into testable features for Google Photos. With guidance from Svitlana Midianko (PM, Google), we learned to balance creativity with real product feasibility.

    Product Management User Research Prototyping Figma UX Design

    The Challenge

    Our refined "How Might We" became:

    How might we design product features inside Google Photos that make online connection feel more meaningful?

    Why this matters: Text-only communication often misses non-verbal cues (tone, expressions, body language). We dug into research on CMC (computer-mediated communication) and leaned on the idea that visuals + emotion can restore some of that lost richness.

    The Process

    We worked like PMs‚Äîevidence-driven, iterative, and always focused on user needs and technical feasibility.

    1. Benchmarking the Landscape

    We studied BeReal and Yubo to see how "authentic sharing" and "interest-based discovery" create social pull‚Äîthen used a gap-analysis lens to imagine Photos-native twists.

    2. Early Concepts ‚Üí Wireframes

    We brainstormed four directions (Video Comments on Photos, Music-Based Memories, Interactive Photo Challenges, and Live Photo sessions), split ownership, sketched drafts, then standardized them into Figma wireframes to test ideas‚Äînot pixels.

    3. User Research

    We ran an HSR-approved survey with 23 Minerva students, gathered qualitative and quantitative feedback, and used it to pick the highest-signal paths forward.

    4. Narrowing Scope

    User feedback favored Live Photo Sharing Sessions; I brought forward the Music-Based Memory Sorting concept, and our PM mentor encouraged us to pursue it for its emotional resonance and partnership upside. We focused on those two.

    5. Prototyping & Iteration

    We turned wireframes into a clickable prototype that looks and feels like Google Photos on Pixel 8. We updated navigation, iterated on flows and microcopy based on testing, resulting in ~25% higher task completion rate across core flows in prototype usability tests (vs. baseline).

    The Solution

    We took two features to high-fidelity prototype, each addressing a different aspect of emotional connection through photos.

    Interactive demo: View Prototype ‚Ä¢ Design file

    üéµ Music-Based Memory Sorting

    The idea: Photos aren't just visuals‚Äîthey're tied to moments. We sort memories by the song playing when a photo/video happened, so re-viewing becomes emotionally sticky.

    Key Features:

    • Home hero with featured memory + "Open playlist on Spotify" CTA
    • Dedicated Music tab with genre/artist/mood sorting
    • Album view with "Link playlist" option and branded partner integration
    • Spotify bridge using EXIF/metadata + Google's ML classification for auto-suggestions

    PM Insight: This feature deepens emotional recall and gives Photos a recurring habit loop. It also opens partnership surface area that aligns with how users already soundtrack their lives.

    üë• Live Photo Sharing Sessions

    The idea: Host a real-time slideshow with friends/family; attendees react and comment live‚Äîturning passive albums into shared moments.

    Key Features:

    • New Share surface with session creation flow
    • Real-time reactions and comments during playback
    • Simple, low-friction interface that maintains focus on media
    • "Watch together" patterns without becoming a social network

    PM Insight: This pushes Photos toward lightweight social presence without fighting core identity. It keeps the center of gravity on your media, not on becoming another feed.

    Reflection & Next Steps

    We learned (sometimes the hard way) to set ambitious but feasible scopes and keep iteration velocity high. Still, we shipped a coherent prototype, rehearsed the story, and left with a clear roadmap for "v2" improvements.

    What I'd Do Next

    • Run focused usability tests with target user segments beyond our student cohort
    • Build a scrappy data plan with events and success metrics to validate impact
    • Draft a partnership RFC outlining the Spotify interface, permissions, and data exchange
    • Explore additional music service integrations (Apple Music, YouTube Music)
    • Test monetization models and partnership revenue opportunities

    Ecosystem Fit: The UI follows Photos' visual language and Pixel ergonomics. Features sit naturally in Home/Library/Share/Music without fighting core information architecture.
